Privacy Policy

Last updated: February 9, 2026

Pin craft ("we", "our", or "us") operates the website hosted on GitHub Pages (the "Service"). This page informs you of our policies regarding the collection, use, and disclosure of personal data when you use our Service and the choices you have associated with that data.

Information We Collect

We do not collect, store, or transmit any personal information on our servers. All interactions occur directly in your browser.

  • Authentication data: When you connect your Pinterest account, we redirect you to Pinterest's OAuth 2.0 authorization page. We receive only a temporary authorization code in the browser URL. No tokens or personal data are stored by Pin craft.
  • Access tokens: If tokens are handled (in future versions), they remain in your browser session only and are never sent to or stored on our side.
  • Usage data: GitHub Pages may log standard HTTP request information (IP, browser, referrer), which is governed by GitHub's privacy policy.

How We Use Your Data

We use authorization codes solely to demonstrate Pinterest API connectivity in the browser. We do not:

  • Store your Pinterest credentials or tokens
  • Sell, share, or disclose your data to third parties
  • Use analytics or tracking scripts beyond GitHub Pages defaults

Third-Party Services

This Service interacts with the Pinterest API. Pinterest handles all authentication and data access. Refer to the Pinterest Privacy Policy for details on how Pinterest processes your data.

Security

We implement reasonable measures (such as PKCE in OAuth flows) to help protect your interaction with the Pinterest API, but remember that no method of transmission over the internet is 100% secure.

Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this policy from time to time. We will notify you of any changes by posting the new Privacy Policy on this page and updating the "Last updated" date.

Contact Us

If you have any questions about this Privacy Policy, please contact us via your preferred channel or open an issue on the associated GitHub repository.
